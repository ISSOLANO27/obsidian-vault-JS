### **Highly Composite** or **Factor-Rich**.

- A **highly composite number** is one that has more divisors than any smaller positive integer.
    
- Numbers like 960 are especially convenient in design because they break down evenly into many factors (2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 12, etc.).
    
- In practical terms, designers often call this **divisibility** or say the number has a **flexible factorization**, since it allows clean splits into halves, thirds, quarters, and so on without messy fractions.
