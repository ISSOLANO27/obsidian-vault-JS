Networks are classified based on the range of devices they cover. 

**[LAN]([What is LAN (Local Area Network)? Understanding LAN's Working, Types and Features - GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/computer-networks/what-is-lan-local-area-network/))** (Local Area Network): A network that is confined within a geographic area such as the confinement of a building. 

**[WAN]([What is a WAN? - Wide-Area Network - Cisco](https://www.cisco.com/site/us/en/learn/topics/networking/what-is-a-wan-wide-area-network.html))** (Wide Area Network): one that covers a wider area. Ex. Several buildings/cities. Usually consists of 2 or more interconnected LANs.

The largest WAN in existence is the **Internet** which contains an almost uncountable number of networks and hosts involving computers.
