
In all the major browsers, these debugging tools are accessible through the same pane that opens with the console. 

The tools can: 
- **track and find** errors in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript code
- **gauge** a page’s **performance and response** over different connections
- **preview** the page’s appearance for **various devices** and **screen resolutions**.

Each browser is slightly different in how it organizes and presents these debugging tools, so you **have to spend some time** evaluating your browser’s debugging environment to fully utilize its features.

|BROWSER|KEYBOARD SHORTCUT|MENU STEPS|
|---|---|---|
|Google Chrome|SHIFT + CTRL + J (Windows)<br><br>Option + ⌘ + J (Macintosh)|1. Click the Chrome menu in the upper-right corner<br>    <br>2. Click More tools<br>    <br>3. Click Developer tools<br>    <br>4. Click Sources from the Developer pane|
|Safari|Option + ⌘ + C|1. Enable the Developer tab by going into the Safari Menu > Preferences window, going to the Advanced tab and selecting Show Develop menu in menu bar checkbox<br>    <br>2. Click the Develop tab in the Safari menu<br>    <br>3. Click Show JavaScript Console<br>    <br>4. Click the Debugger tab|
|Microsoft Edge|F12 and then click Console from the Developer pane menu|1. Click the Edge menu in the upper-right corner<br>    <br>2. Click More tools<br>    <br>3. Click Developer tools<br>    <br>4. Click Sources from the Developer pane|
|Firefox|SHIFT + CTRL + J (Windows)<br><br>Option + ⌘ + J (Macintosh)|1. Click the Firefox menu in the upper-right corner<br>    <br>2. Click Web Developer<br>    <br>3. Click Debugger|
