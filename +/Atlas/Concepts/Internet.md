The **Internet** is the global system that makes your browser useful — it’s the underlying network that connects computers, servers, and devices worldwide. Think of it as the “roads and highways” that let information travel, while the browser is your “car” that drives on those roads.

---

## 🔑 What the Internet Is

- A **network of networks** — millions of smaller networks (home Wi-Fi, corporate LANs, mobile networks) linked together.
- Uses a common set of rules called the **Internet Protocol Suite (TCP/IP)** to let machines communicate.
- Provides the infrastructure for services like the **World Wide Web, email, file transfer, streaming, and messaging**. [Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/technology/Internet)

---

## 📊 How It Works

1. **You request something** (like typing a URL in your [[web browser]]).
2. **Your device sends the request** through your local network to your Internet Service Provider (ISP).
3. **Routers and servers pass the request** across the global network until it reaches the destination server.
4. **The server responds** with data (HTML, CSS, images, etc.).
5. **Your browser interprets and displays** that data as a web page. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet)

---

## 🧠 Why It Matters

- The Internet is not a single machine or place — it’s a **decentralized system**.
- It enables global communication, commerce, education, entertainment, and research.
- Without it, browsers would have nothing to fetch or display.

---

## 🚀 Key Takeaway

The Internet is the **global backbone of digital communication** — a vast, decentralized network that connects billions of devices. Your browser is the tool that makes sense of the raw data flowing through it. [sciencenewstoday.org](https://www.sciencenewstoday.org/what-is-the-internet-a-complete-guide-to-how-it-works-and-why-it-matters)

---

👉 In short: The Internet is the infrastructure; the browser is the interpreter. One provides the roads, the other drives on them.
