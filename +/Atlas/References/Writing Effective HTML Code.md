Be aware of the[[ Requirements Of Various Browsers And Devices]], as well as understanding the different [[Versions Of The Language (HTML)]].

Guidelines:
1. Know the History and its various versions (HTML and XHTML). The history does *impact your code*.

2. Know your market

	>Do you have to support older browsers? Or are your clients in agreement with a Browser and/or Version?
	
	>Will your web pages be seen on a single device? Or do you have to support a variety devices?
	
3.	Test Your Code! Do it on several browsers and versions. Do not assume!!

	> Also, check on the speed of the connection. A large file that performs well on a high-speed connection might be unusable in a case of a slower connection.
	

To effectively communicate with customers and users, you need to make sure your website content is **always** readable. Writing good HTML code is a great place to start.
