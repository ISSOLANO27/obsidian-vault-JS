[**Client-Server Network**](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/system-design/client-server-model/) is a common network in which the clients have access to information provided by one or more servers.

Also known as a `two-tier system`.

**Server**: The host that provides information or a service to other devices on the network.

**Client (front-end)**: The device receiving the service.

---

To be successful in web development you really need to understand the [[Client-Server Model]].

Problem: Early web Clients were weak, Browsers were slow, and machines underpowered.

Solution: Safest place to do the real work was the [[Roles of the Server]].
